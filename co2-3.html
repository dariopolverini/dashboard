CO₂ Storage in Concrete

From Biomass to Carbon Sequestration

Biomass absorbs CO₂ from the atmosphere through photosynthesis. When heated in an oxygen-free pyrolysis unit, the biomass decomposes into biochar—a stable, carbon-rich material resembling coal. Biochar's resistance to degradation makes it ideal for long-term carbon sequestration.

Enhancing Concrete with Biochar

Mixed into concrete, biochar's porous structure improves strength and durability while permanently embedding carbon in the material. Once integrated into the concrete matrix, the carbon remains trapped, preventing CO₂ release and creating a durable carbon sink.

Project Application

For the 3D-printed concrete elements in this project, biochar was incorporated into the mix at a ratio of 1.5% of cement weight. This integration enhances the material's performance while contributing to carbon sequestration.

The use of biochar-modified concrete achieves a 5% reduction in net emissions during production, further advancing the sustainability of the construction process.
