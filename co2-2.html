CO₂ Emission Reduction

Baseline Emissions of the Tankering Solution

A typical large diesel-powered tanker truck consumes 30 liters of diesel per 100 km (0.3 liters per kilometer). Diesel fuel produces approximately 2.68 kg of CO₂ per liter, meaning a 30-cubic-meter tanker truck emits 0.8 kg of CO₂ per kilometer traveled.

In the current scenario, wastewater is transported once per week to Al Bada, covering a distance of 65.8 km, resulting in 52.64 kg of CO₂ per trip. Similarly, irrigation water is brought back from Al Bada with the same frequency and distance, adding another 52.64 kg of CO₂ per trip.

This results in a total annual CO₂ emission of 5.3 tons of CO₂e per year.

Solution

By implementing a decentralized wastewater treatment solution, the need for tankering is eliminated, leading to a complete removal of these emissions and a more sustainable, self-sufficient water management system.
