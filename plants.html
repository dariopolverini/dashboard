Innovation Hub Garden

Plant Selection Criteria

The plant selection prioritizes native and locally adapted species to ensure resilience and sustainability. These plants require minimal maintenance, are drought-tolerant or need moderate irrigation, and enhance biodiversity by attracting pollinators. Their diverse foliage and vibrant flowers contribute both ecological and aesthetic value.

Sustainable Landscaping

Green landscapes, ornamental gardens, and tree-lined walkways enrich community spaces. However, increasing summer temperatures, water scarcity, and maintenance costs drive the need for sustainable landscaping approaches.

Soil Improvement for Long-Term Sustainability

Specialized sand amendments enhance soil structure, promoting optimal root development, reducing replanting costs, and improving water efficiency. This method achieves up to 50% water savings, contributing to a more sustainable landscape.

Enhanced Oxygenation and Organic Matter Development

The high porosity of breathable sand improves oxygenation, fostering organic matter formation. This self-sustaining soil structure enhances nutrient absorption and moisture distribution, leading to healthier and more resilient plants.
